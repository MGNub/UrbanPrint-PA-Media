S O L U T I O N S

Enhance Sidewalks and Crossings

Build wider, accessible sidewalks with smooth surfaces, ramps, and tactile paving. Install pedestrian crossings with clear signage, countdown timers, and leading pedestrian intervals (LPIs) to prioritize walkers.

Ensure Safety and Accessibility

Install ample street lighting, emergency call boxes, and universal design features like curb cuts and audible signals for pedestrians with disabilities. Develop car-free streets or pedestrian-only plazas in downtown areas to boost foot traffic and support local businesses.

Integrate Mixed-Use Development

Encourage zoning for residential, commercial, and recreational spaces in close proximity to reduce reliance on cars and create vibrant, walkable neighborhoods. Design seamless walking paths to bus or train stops with shelters, clear signage, and bike-sharing stations for last-mile connectivity.

Add Green and Aesthetic Elements

Plant shade trees, create pocket parks, and incorporate public art or murals along walkways to enhance comfort and visual appeal.
